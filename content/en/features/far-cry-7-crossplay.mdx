<Section icon={Target} title="Co-op Mechanics and Gameplay Systems">

The gameplay loop of Project Blackbird introduces dramatic changes to the traditional open-world formula. Instead of liberating outposts in a linear sequence, players must manage a real-time clock displayed on the protagonist's wristwatch. Cooperative play must adapt to these survival-focused mechanics.

### Key Campaign Mechanics

| Mechanic | System Detail | Impact on Co-op Play |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **In-Game Timer** | 72-hour countdown | Shared countdown clock across all connected players |
| **Safehouses** | Pauses active timer | Strategic planning areas to coordinate rescue routes |
| **Interrogations** | Enemies give truth, lies, or escape | Requires players to cross-reference gathered intel |
| **Movement Overhaul** | Tactical sprint, slide, vault | Faster traversal to bypass map hazards together |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Safehouse">
    Enter a safehouse to pause the active 72-hour campaign countdown. This is where you and your co-op partner gear up and select targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Interrogate Targets">
    Track down enemy informants. Players must work together to prevent targets from escaping or feeding the squad false rescue locations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Nonlinear Rescues">
    Infiltrate enemy territory to rescue Beckett family members. Since family members can die, coordinated stealth or heavy assaults are required.
  </Step>
</Steps>
