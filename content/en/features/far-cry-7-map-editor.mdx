<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Survival Map Creation">

Creating a functional survival map in the **Far Cry 7 map editor** requires a different approach than standard outpost missions. You must account for player progression, ammo replenishment, and escalating difficulty.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Define the Combat Arena">
    Start by sculpting a terrain that offers both high-ground advantages and "choke points." For survival mode, the map should be circular or semi-enclosed to keep the action focused. Ensure there are clear sightlines for players but enough cover to survive heavy fire.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure the Spawner Logic">
    Use the new "Endless Wave" node in the logic editor. Set the initial wave to 5-10 low-tier enemies. Create a variable that increases enemy health or damage by 5% every three waves to ensure the challenge scales with player skill.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Place Resource Stations">
    In survival mode, ammo and health are your primary currencies. Place "Buy Stations" or "Refill Crates" in risky locations. This forces players to move around the map rather than camping in a single corner for the entire match.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Set Milestones and Rewards">
    Give players a reason to keep playing. Use the "Milestone" trigger to grant players temporary power-ups or unlock new areas of the map after surviving 10, 20, or 50 waves. This mimics the addictive loops found in classic survival shooters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Cross-Platform Testing">
    Before publishing, use the "Multi-Platform Simulation" tool to see how your map performs on different hardware. Once satisfied, hit the publish button to send your creation to the global Far Cry 7 network.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Milestone Rewards">
Adding world records and global leaderboards to your survival maps significantly increases player engagement. Creators who implement "Reach Wave 100" challenges often see their maps stay in the "Trending" section for months.
</Callout>

</Section>
