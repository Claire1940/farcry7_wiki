<Section icon={Swords} title="The 72-Hour Timer and Interrogation Mechanics">

The gameplay loop of the next mainline game introduces a drastic shift from traditional open-world structures. Rather than clearing outposts in a linear fashion, players operate under a strict in-game clock to rescue the kidnapped Beckett family.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Manage the Countdown">
    Players must monitor a visible wristwatch showing a 72-hour countdown (roughly 24 real-time hours). Active exploration and combat deplete this timer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Safehouses">
    Entering a discovered safehouse pauses the countdown, allowing players to upgrade gear, review clues, and plan their next route without pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Interrogate Hostiles">
    Enemies can be interrogated for coordinates of captive family members. However, targets may lie, refuse to speak, or attempt to escape.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Rescue">
    Infiltrate enemy strongholds to free family members. The order in which you rescue them is entirely up to you, altering the story's outcome.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Tactical Advantage">
Interrogating high-ranking cult members yields more reliable intelligence, but forces you to expend precious time tracking them down across the map.
</Callout>

| Interrogation Outcome | Player Impact | Risk Level | Time Cost |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Truthful Coordinates** | Direct path to captive | Low | Minimal |
| **Deceptive Leads** | Leads to ambush / traps | High | Significant |
| **Target Silence** | No information gained | Medium | Moderate |
| **Target Escapes** | Alerts nearby outposts | High | High |

</Section>
