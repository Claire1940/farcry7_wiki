<Section icon={Map} title="How the Snowdrop Engine Redefines the Arsenal">

For the first time in the franchise's history, Ubisoft is reportedly moving away from the Dunia engine to the **Snowdrop engine** (the technology powering *The Division* and *Star Wars Outlaws*). This engine swap introduces massive changes to weapon handling, ballistic physics, and environmental interactions. 

### Key Technical Improvements in Gunplay
- **Realistic Ballistics**: Bullets experience realistic drop and penetration based on the caliber and target material.
- **Dynamic Destruction**: Cover is no longer permanent. Heavy caliber weapons can shred wooden fences, drywall, and thin metal sheets.
- **Weapon Wear and Tear**: Scavenged weapons from conspiracy cultists may jam or show decreased accuracy until cleaned or repaired.

| Engine Feature | Dunia Engine (Old) | Snowdrop Engine (New) | Combat Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bullet Physics** | Hitscan / Basic Projectile | Full Ballistic Simulation | Bullet drop affects long-range snipers |
| **Cover System** | Static / Indestructible | Dynamic / Destructible | Cover degrades under heavy enemy fire |
| **Weapon Handling** | Arcade-style recoil | Procedural recoil & kick | Sustained fire requires active control |
