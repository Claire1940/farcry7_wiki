<Section icon={Zap} title="Gameplay Mechanics: The 72-Hour Timer Explained">

The most controversial aspect of the **far cry 7 leaks** is the implementation of a real-time pressure mechanic. The game features an in-game countdown of 72 hours. While this sounds restrictive, the system is designed to create tension rather than frustration.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Countdown">
    The 72-hour in-game clock translates to approximately 24 hours of real-world gameplay. This timer is visible on the protagonist's wristwatch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Safehouse Pausing">
    The timer stops whenever the player enters a safehouse. This allows players to manage their inventory, craft gear, and plan their next move without losing precious time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Intel Gathering">
    Through the interrogation system, players find the locations of family members. A successful interrogation might reveal a hidden bunker, but a failed one could lead to a trap.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Rescue or Loss">
    If the timer reaches zero before a family member is found, they are considered lost. The game continues, but the narrative and difficulty adapt to these losses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Technical Shift: Dunia to Snowdrop Engine">

For over a decade, the Far Cry series has relied on the Dunia Engine. However, **far cry 7 leaks** confirm a massive technical migration to the **Snowdrop Engine**, the same technology powering *The Division* and *Star Wars Outlaws*.

**Benefits of the Snowdrop Transition:**
- **Improved Physics**: Better environmental destruction and object interaction.
- **Advanced AI**: More complex NPC behaviors during interrogations and stealth.
- **Seamless Loading**: Faster transitions between the open world and safehouses.
- **Lighting**: Enhanced volumetric effects, crucial for the cult's hallucinogenic sequences.
