<Section icon={Target} title="How the 72-Hour Timer and Interrogation Systems Work">

The most radical departure from classic Far Cry gameplay is the implementation of a persistent campaign timer. The **far cry 7 story** unfolds over a strict time limit, forcing players to make tactical decisions about when to fight, when to search for clues, and when to rest.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The 72-Hour Countdown">
    The campaign features a 72-hour in-game timer, which translates to roughly 24 real-time hours. Players must track this directly on their character's wristwatch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Interrogating Enemies">
    To locate family members, players must capture and interrogate enemies. However, targets do not always cooperate; they can lie, stay silent, give false directions, or attempt to escape.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilizing Safehouses">
    Entering a safehouse pauses the active countdown timer. This allows players to manage their inventory, craft gear, and plan their next rescue route safely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Living with Consequences">
    If the timer expires or a rescue mission goes horribly wrong, family members can die. The story continues regardless, but the loss permanently alters the ending.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Gameplay Balance">
The timer mechanic is designed to create tension, but safehouses ensure players are not rushed during crucial planning phases. This balances survival mechanics with traditional open-world exploration.
</Callout>

This system ensures that no two playthroughs of the campaign will be exactly alike. A failed interrogation could lead you to a dead end, wasting precious hours while your relatives remain in danger.

| Mechanic | In-Game Value | Strategic Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Campaign Clock | 72 hours (In-Game) | Creates narrative tension and limits passive waiting |
| Safehouse Pause | Infinite until exit | Provides strategic breaks to plan routes and craft |
| Interrogation Outcomes | 4 distinct reactions | Forces players to verify information before acting |

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Project Blackbird vs. Project Maverick">

It is crucial to distinguish between the two Far Cry projects currently in development at Ubisoft. While Project Blackbird contains the narrative-driven, timed rescue campaign of Far Cry 7, **Project Maverick** is a separate, multiplayer-focused experience.

| Feature | Project Blackbird (Far Cry 7) | Project Maverick |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Focus** | Single-player / Narrative campaign | Multiplayer extraction shooter |
| **Setting** | Unconfirmed main map (Beckett estate/cult grounds) | Fictional Alaskan wilderness |
| **Core Objective** | Rescue family members before time runs out | Gather loot, complete contracts, extract safely |
| **Permadeath** | Family members can die; protagonist persists | Carried gear and perks are lost upon death |
| **Game Engine** | Snowdrop Engine | Snowdrop Engine |

<Callout type="success" title="Shared Technology">
Although they are separate games, both projects originated from a shared prototype called Talisker. They share updated movement mechanics, including tactical sprinting, sliding, vaulting, and an overhauled looting system.
</Callout>

While Project Maverick caters to the competitive extraction crowd, Project Blackbird remains the true successor for fans of deep, atmospheric single-player campaigns.

</Section>
