<Section icon={Map} title="How the 72-Hour Timer Affects the Cast">

The most revolutionary change in Far Cry 7 is the reported **72-hour in-game timer** (roughly 24 hours of real-time play). This mechanic creates a "living" cast. In previous games, characters would wait forever for you to complete a mission. In Far Cry 7, the clock is always ticking.

**Timer Mechanics and Character Outcomes:**
- **Safehouse Pauses:** When you enter a safehouse, the timer stops, allowing you to plan your next move with your rescued cast members.
- **Failed Rescues:** If you take too long to reach a Beckett family member, they may be moved to a more dangerous location or killed.
- **Dynamic Dialogue:** Rescued family members will react to the deaths of others, creating a highly reactive script.

| Action | Impact on Timer | Impact on Cast |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Resting at Safehouse** | Paused | No immediate change. |
| **Successful Interrogation** | Continues | Reveals location of a family member. |
| **Failed Rescue Attempt** | Continues | Potential death of a family character. |
| **Exploration/Looting** | Continues | Reduces time available for the main cast rescue. |
