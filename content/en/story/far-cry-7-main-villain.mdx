<Section icon={Target} title="The Beckett Family and Nonlinear Campaign Structure">

Instead of liberating a vast nation or leading a regional rebel army, the narrative focus of the next Far Cry campaign is deeply personal. Players control a protagonist tasked with rescuing their wealthy, kidnapped relatives—the **Beckett Family**—who have been targeted directly by the Sons of Truth. 

This rescue operation introduces a highly dynamic, nonlinear campaign. Rather than completing a fixed sequence of story missions, players must explore the open world to locate and extract family members in any order they choose.

<Section icon={Swords} title="How the Interrogation and Timer Systems Work">

To locate the kidnapped Beckett family members, players cannot rely on simple map markers. Instead, you must actively gather intelligence by capturing and questioning members of the Sons of Truth. The interrogation system is designed to be highly unpredictable, forcing players to make quick tactical judgments.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Capture High-Value Targets">
    Infiltrate Sons of Truth outposts and subdue officers or informants alive instead of eliminating them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Initiate Interrogation">
    Question the target to extract coordinates, safehouse locations, or details regarding family members.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assess the Information">
    Analyze the target's response. Informants can give accurate directions, lie, stay completely silent, or attempt to escape.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Rescue">
    Follow the lead before the 72-hour in-game countdown (approximately 24 real-time hours) runs out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Gameplay Optimization">
Interrogation outcomes are influenced by your approach. Using stealth to isolate targets and securing the immediate area increases the likelihood of extracting truthful coordinates.
</Callout>
