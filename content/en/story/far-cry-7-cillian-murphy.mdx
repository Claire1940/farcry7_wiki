<Section icon={Clock} title="The 72-Hour Timer and Gameplay Mechanics">

The most controversial and innovative feature reported for Far Cry 7 is the **72-hour in-game timer**. This mechanic forces players to manage their time efficiently as they attempt to locate and rescue kidnapped members of the Beckett family. In real-world time, this translates to approximately **24 hours of gameplay**, though the clock can be manipulated through specific actions.

**How the Timer System Works:**
- **Active Countdown**: The timer runs while exploring the open world and engaging in combat.
- **Safehouse Pause**: Entering a designated safehouse pauses the countdown, allowing players to plan their next move.
- **Interrogation Gains**: Successfully extracting information from enemies can reveal family locations, saving precious hours.
- **Consequences**: If the timer reaches zero before family members are rescued, the story concludes with a "failed" ending.

| **Tactical Sprint** | Faster Traversal | Use for quick escapes to save time |
| **Safehouse Resting** | Pauses Time Limit | Always resupply at safehouses to stop the clock |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Story and The Beckett Family Rescue">

The narrative of Far Cry 7 moves away from the traditional "revolutionary" setup. Instead of liberating a country, you are saving a wealthy family—the Becketts—from a conspiracy cult. This cult, the **Sons of Truth**, believes in a coming societal collapse and uses hallucinogens to indoctrinate their followers.
