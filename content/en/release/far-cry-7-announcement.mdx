<Section icon={Target} title="Gameplay Mechanics: The 72-Hour Timer">

The most discussed feature following the **far cry 7 announcement** rumors is the introduction of a persistent in-game timer. The campaign is structured around a 72-hour window in which you must complete the primary rescue objectives. In real-world time, this translates to approximately 24 hours of gameplay. This mechanic is designed to create a sense of urgency and force players to make difficult decisions about which leads to follow.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Start the Countdown">
    The timer begins as soon as the main campaign kicks off. You can track the remaining time via an in-game wristwatch worn by the protagonist.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Tactical Scouting">
    Explore the open world to find clues. Movement is more tactical now, utilizing sliding and vaulting to navigate hostile environments quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Rescue Operation">
    Locate a family member and initiate a rescue. Success stops their individual "danger" status, but the overall campaign clock continues to tick.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Safehouse Management">
    Enter a designated safehouse to pause the timer. This is where you upgrade gear, plan your next route, and manage your inventory without losing precious hours.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Time Management">
The timer only pauses when you are inside a safehouse. If you spend too much time wandering or engaging in non-essential side activities, you risk the permanent death of family members.
</Callout>

This system is a massive shift from the "play at your own pace" style of Far Cry 6. It introduces a rogue-lite element where efficiency is rewarded. While some fans are concerned about the pressure, Ubisoft reportedly intends for this to increase replayability, as different playthroughs will result in different family survivors and story outcomes.

</Section>
